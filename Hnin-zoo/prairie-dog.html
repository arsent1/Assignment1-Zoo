Prairie Dog

Prairie dogs are resilient creatures known for their toughness, with sharp claws and teeth. They are cousins of backyard squirrels and share a family with groundhogs, chipmunks, marmots, and woodchucks. Prairie Dogs have a unique mating season lasting only an hour each year, resulting in small litters. They live in close-knit family groups called coteries, with females staying together for life. They also play a crucial role in benefiting around 150 other species by creating habitat islands. They serve as a food source for various animals and help fertilize the soil. They also communicate through a very advanced language system that allows them to convey specific details about approaching threats. Unfortunately, their populations continued to decline due to habitat destruction and disease in the 1900s but conservation efforts are working to protect both species and preserve their fragile habitats.

Diet

  • Grass
  • Forbs
  • Seeds
